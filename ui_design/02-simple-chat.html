Quantum entanglement is a phenomenon where two or more particles become correlated in such a way that the quantum state of each particle cannot be described independently 1.

How it works

  • Two particles interact and become entangled
  • Measuring one particle instantly determines the correlated property of the other
  • This holds true regardless of the distance between them

Can information travel faster than light?

No. The No-Communication Theorem 2 proves this rigorously. The key reason is that measurement outcomes are random — you cannot control what result you get, so you cannot encode a message in it.
