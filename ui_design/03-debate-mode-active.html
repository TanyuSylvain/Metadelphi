📝 Expert Answer v1.0 · confidence 88%
Consciousness is most parsimoniously explained by physical brain processes. Key evidence: (1) anesthesia reliably abolishes consciousness by affecting neural activity; (2) split-brain studies show two independent streams of awareness from one person; (3) no verified case of consciousness without a brain exists. The "hard problem" remains philosophically open, but lacks empirical support for non-physical causation.
